I am a Senior Fellow (Postdoc) in Biomedical and Health Informatics at the University of Washington. My research is focused on informatics for synthetic biologists. I work on the Synthetic Biology Information Lifecycle Management for the Enterprise project with Professors John Gennari and Herbert Sauro in collaboration with Dr. Evren Sirin of Clark and Parsia, LLC.

I completed my PhD in Biomedical and Health Informatics at the University of Washington in 2012. As part of my dissertation research I co-founded the Synthetic Biology Open Language (SBOL) an information exchange standard for biological engineering. I now continues to serve as an SBOL Editor.

I had the privilege of being advised by John H. Gennari, and worked with Herbert M Sauro, and Daniel L. Cook. My previous research at the Children’s Hospital Boston was on the genetic basis of Autism. I received a BS in Biology from the University of Maryland in 2002 and a MS in Bioinformatics from Boston University in 2005.

For the most up-to-date information: http://www.linkedin.com/in/mgaldzic








Research Interests

Synthetic biology is the emerging research and engineering field concerned with the design and construction of new biological functions and systems.

My current focus is to understand how information technology could help synthetic biologists engineer biological systems

I aim to use this understanding to develop an open and distributed repository architecture and services for knowledge sharing in synthetic biology.

Active Projects

SUBLIME

The aim of this project is to develop a software tool for the management of information throughout the synthetic biology research lifecycle. The capabilities of this tool will include version control, intelligent information retrieval about synthetic biology components, and configuration management to assist in assembling those components into working biological systems. We will design, develop, and test this tool in direct collaboration with working synthetic biologists from Dr. Sauro's laboratory.

Synthetic Biology Open Language (SBOL)

SBOL is a data exchange standard for descriptions of genetic parts, devices, modules, and systems.

     SBOL is designed to aid the following cases:
  • Allow synthetic biologists and genetic engineers to electronically exchange designs with round-tripping*.
  • Send and receive genetic designs to and from biofabrication centers.
  • Facilitate storage of genetic designs in repositories.
  • Embed genetic designs in publications.
